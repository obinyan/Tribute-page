Abstract

The curriculum for Design and Technology in secondary schools England and Wales has been under review. With policy makers questioning not only the position the subject occupies within the curriculum but also the value Design and Technologies holds. As a result, Design and Technologies future, as a subject, is uncertain. Set against a background of policy and curriculum change, this paper presents the findings of a research study designed to elicit the perceptions and gain an insight into the way key stage 3 pupils (11-14 years) view Design and Technology. Utilizing the concept of the original PATT tools and building upon the work of previous studies undertaken nationally or globally, the fundamental aim of the research at inception was to investigate the perceptions of pupils with respect to their understanding of what is technology education.
